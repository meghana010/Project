E-adoption is a type of adoption where a person or a family supports a child in need by providing financial and emotional assistance. This assistance can go towards the child's education, healthcare, housing, and other needs. The child remains living with their biological family or in a foster care facility, and the adoptive family usually receives updates on the child's progress and well-being. Virtual adoption is a way for families who cannot adopt in the traditional sense to still make a difference in the life of a child in need. While virtual adoption can have positive effects on children's lives, it should not be confused with formal adoption. Virtual adoption does not grant legal custody or guardianship, nor does it provide the same level of stability and permanency for the child.
